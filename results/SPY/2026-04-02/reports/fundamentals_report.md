## 1. Company / Fund Profile

The **SPDR S&P 500 ETF Trust (SPY)** is one of the world's largest and most liquid exchange-traded funds, managed by **State Street Global Advisors**. SPY was launched on **January 22, 1993**, making it the first ETF listed in the United States. Its objective is to replicate the performance of the **S&P 500 Index**, which tracks 500 of the largest publicly traded companies in the U.S. across all major sectors of the economy.

### Key Characteristics:
- **Type:** Passively Managed Exchange-Traded Fund (ETF)
- **Benchmark:** S&P 500 Index
- **Issuer:** State Street Global Advisors (SSGA)
- **Structure:** Unit Investment Trust (UIT)
- **Currency:** USD
- **Primary Exchange:** NYSE Arca

> **Note:** As an ETF/UIT, SPY does not have its own balance sheet, income statement, or cash flow statement in the traditional corporate sense. Its financial statements reflect underlying index holdings and trust operations, not operating revenues or liabilities in the way an individual company would report.

## 3. Fund Structure & Financial Note

Since SPY operates as a **Unit Investment Trust (UIT)**, traditional corporate financial statements (balance sheet, income statement, cash flow statement) are **not applicable**. Instead, the fund's "financials" are represented through:

- **Net Asset Value (NAV):** The per-share fair value of the underlying S&P 500 portfolio.
- **Expense Ratio:** SPY charges a **0.0945% gross expense ratio** (one of the lowest among large ETFs), which minimizes drag on returns versus the benchmark.
- **Distributions:** Quarterly dividend distributions paid from income received on held securities.
- **Trust Assets:** The trust holds shares in all 500 S&P 500 companies in proportion to their index weight. The top holdings (as of recent data) are typically Apple (AAPL), Microsoft (MSFT), NVIDIA (NVDA), Amazon (AMZN), Alphabet (GOOGL), and Meta (META), which together account for a significant portion of the fund.

### The Bear Case Anchors

Three converging factors support the short:

1. **Descending channel / lower highs structure.** The sequence of collapsing highs is observable price structure, not narrative. Within a 1h horizon, the path of least resistance respects the dominant structure until a clean break proves otherwise.

2. **Trapped longs creating overhead supply.** The mass of buyers underwater between $80K and $97K (or equivalent overhead in whatever the current regime is) creates real supply pressure on every rally. This is basic supply-demand mechanics, not speculation.

3. **Low-volume consolidation below resistance.** The Neutral Analyst's volume normalization argument is the sharpest analytical point in the entire debate. Below-average volume at resistance on a 1h chart reads as exhaustion or distribution, not accumulation coiling for a launch.

The $68,500 invalidation is non-negotiable. If BTC closes an hourly candle above that level on expanding volume, the Bollinger squeeze thesis goes to the bull side and the position exits immediately. The entire edge of this trade is the tight stop relative to the $64,000–$65,000 target — roughly a 1:4.5 risk-reward ratio if entry is achieved at $67,750. That ratio only holds if the stop is respected.

Small size is not timidity — it is the correct structural response to a coiled-spring environment where the breakout direction is genuinely uncertain. The bear edge is real but narrow. Size accordingly.

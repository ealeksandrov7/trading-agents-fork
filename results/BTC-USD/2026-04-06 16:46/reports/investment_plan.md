I’m siding with the bear analyst: Sell BTC-USD.

The bull case is still the same strong narrative: fixed supply, “digital gold,” institutional access via ETFs, and the idea that adoption plus scarcity can keep supporting price. That matters over longer cycles.

But for a 1h trading horizon, the bear argument is more actionable and convincing: BTC-USD still behaves like a liquidity-driven, high-beta risk asset, not a stable reserve. Without a cash-flow anchor, short-term price is much more vulnerable to sentiment and positioning shifts. If buyers hesitate, there’s no fundamental valuation floor to stop a fast air pocket. That makes downside more tradable than upside here unless price proves otherwise.

My recommendation is to tactically short weakness, not blindly chase. The best setup is a break below the most recent 1h swing low or a failed retest of that broken support. Enter on rejection, with a stop just above the failed-retest high or the prior 1h lower high. Take partial profits at the recent range low or 1R, then trail the rest toward 2R if momentum accelerates.

If BTC-USD instead reclaims the prior 1h range high and holds above it for at least one candle with clear momentum, that invalidates the short thesis quickly; exit immediately and do not fight it.

So the plan is simple: sell failed strength or breakdown continuation in BTC-USD, keep risk tight, and treat any reclaim of near-term resistance as a hard stop.

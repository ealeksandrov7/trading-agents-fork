- **De-escalation Oscillations**: Markets swung dramatically between risk-off and risk-on modes throughout the week based on evolving conflict signals. On Tuesday and Wednesday, "de-escalation hopes" lifted SPY ~0.8-1.0% pre-bell each day.
- **Oil above $100/barrel**: This is a **significant inflationary threshold**, complicating the Fed's already delicate policy balancing act.

## 3. 📉 Federal Reserve & Inflation: Powell's Supply Shock Warning

**Fed Chair Jerome Powell** issued a stark warning that markets cannot overlook:

> *"Now we have another supply shock coming."*

- Powell framed this as a sequential threat: after inflation was brought down in 2023–2024, a **new supply-side inflationary shock** is approaching — likely driven by oil prices, geopolitical supply chain disruptions, and energy costs.
- This severely **limits the Fed's ability to cut rates**, as doing so amid a supply shock would risk re-igniting inflation.
- The Fed is effectively **stuck in a stagflationary dilemma**: slowing growth on one hand, persistent inflation on the other.

> **Actionable Insight**: Rate cut expectations should be **revised downward**. Bond markets and rate-sensitive equities (REITs, utilities, growth tech) face continued headwinds. This is a **bearish signal for SPY** in the medium term.

- The **divergence** between small and large employers could indicate early signs of corporate caution in the face of geopolitical uncertainty and rising input costs.
